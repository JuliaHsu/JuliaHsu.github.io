Biography

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Information Technology with a concentration in Information Sciences and Technology at the School of Computing, George Mason University. My research focuses on computational social science and information sciences, where I apply data-driven and computational methods to study social phenomena and improve information access. My broader interests include artificial intelligence for social good, community informatics, and civic technology.

I am currently a Graduate Research Assistant at the Community Informatics Lab, where I collaborate on projects that leverage computational approaches to address societal challenges.
